# Blueprint Books Brief

Prepared for: Raves Inc. / Raves Construction  
Date: June 9, 2026  
Purpose: decide whether to run job-costed construction books inside Blueprint.

## Executive Summary

Blueprint already carries the work: won quotes, vendor bills, customers, jobs, and attachments. The financial picture of that work still lives in spreadsheets and separate accounting software, so job profit, A/R, and A/P are always a step behind.

**Blueprint Books** puts a real set of construction books in the same platform the team already uses. It is double-entry accounting tailored for construction: a job-costed chart of accounts, invoices and A/R, vendor bills and A/P, financial statements, and a lockable month-end close.

Decision request:

> Approve a fixed-scope Blueprint Books Foundation build, with the SOW confirming price, timeline, the opening-balance source, and acceptance criteria.

Why now:

> The money already flows through Blueprint — the loaded quote log shows about $9.3M marked open across 536 records, and vendor bills and POs are already arriving as task attachments. Today that information is re-keyed into spreadsheets and accounting software after the fact. Blueprint Books removes the re-keying and makes profit, receivables, and payables current.

## What Is Already Built

The accounting engine is already in Blueprint and demoable today:

- **Construction chart of accounts** — retainage receivable and payable, costs and billings in excess (WIP), change-order revenue, and job-cost categories for materials, subs, labor, equipment, and permits.
- **Invoices and A/R** — invoice customers (including from won quotes), record payments, and watch A/R aging.
- **Bills and A/P** — enter vendor bills, record payments, and watch A/P aging.
- **Ledger and trial balance** — double-entry journal with a live trial balance that ties out to the penny.
- **Financial statements** — income statement and balance sheet on demand.
- **Period close** — lock the books through a date once a month is final.
- **Branded PDFs** — invoice and bill PDFs.

This phase is not building accounting from scratch. It tailors what is built, loads Raves opening balances, wires it to your real customers and vendors, and rolls it out to your team.

## Recommended Phase: Books Foundation

### Outcome

Job-costed books that live where the work lives, so leadership sees current profit, receivables, and payables without waiting on a separate system.

### Proposed Scope

- Tailor the construction chart of accounts to how Raves runs jobs.
- Load opening balances from a recent trial balance or accounting export.
- Wire invoices and customer payments into A/R, with aging.
- Wire vendor bills and payments into A/P, with aging.
- Confirm income statement and balance sheet match expectations.
- Run the first month-end close in Blueprint and lock the period.
- Train the accounting owner and the office team.

### What Raves Provides

- An accounting owner (controller, office manager, owner, or outside bookkeeper).
- Opening balances: a recent trial balance, QuickBooks export, or accountant-provided figures.
- A short chart-of-accounts review so the categories match how Raves wants to see profit.
- Entity and tax basics (entity type, sales-tax handling if applicable).

## Expanded Option: Books Complete

Choose this if Raves wants to fully move off its current accounting software.

Adds to the Foundation scope:

- Bank reconciliation.
- Cash-flow statement.
- Migration of historical books from current accounting software.
- A parallel month run against existing books before going live.
- 30-day hypercare after go-live.

## What It Does And Does Not Do

| Does | Does Not |
| --- | --- |
| Job-costed double-entry books in Blueprint | File taxes |
| Current profit, A/R, and A/P | Replace your accountant or CPA |
| Invoices, bills, payments, statements, close | Run payroll processing |
| Branded invoice and bill PDFs | Bank reconciliation or migration (Books Complete) |

The goal is to give Raves — and your accountant — clean, tied-out books in the same system the work lives in.
